Optimal k Selection

Elbow Method & Silhouette Score

How we determined the number of clusters to use.

Elbow Method (WCSS)

WCSS drops sharply from k=2 to k=4, then flattens out. The bend at k=4 indicates it as the optimal number of clusters.

Silhouette Scores

Silhouette score measures how well each region fits its assigned cluster. Higher = better separation. k=4 is chosen for interpretability.

While k=2 has the highest silhouette score, it produces only 2 groups which is not meaningful for policy purposes. k=4 balances statistical validity with real-world interpretability.

Why k=4?

01
Elbow Method shows a clear inflection point at k=4. Adding more clusters after k=4 yields diminishing improvements in WCSS.
02
Interpretability — 4 clusters map naturally to meaningful real-world risk levels: Critical, High, Moderate, Manageable. This makes the results actionable for BJMP policymakers.
03
k=2 rejected despite highest silhouette score because it only produces 2 broad groups (High and Low) which lacks the granularity needed for meaningful policy differentiation.
04
Final Inertia: 17.1152 with k=4 confirms well-formed, compact clusters across the 18 Philippine regions.
